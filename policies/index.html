Consent Policy

  1. Purpose

    This policy outlines how our clinic obtains informed consent from patients (adults and children) prior to assessment and treatment. It ensures that all patients understand the nature, purpose, benefits and risks of physiotherapy interventions and agree to receive care voluntarily.

  2. Adult Consent

    Definition:

    Adult patients are individuals aged 18 years or older who are capable of making their own healthcare decisions.

    Policy:
    1. Information Provision – Before any treatment, the physiotherapist will explain:
      • The assessment and treatment plan.
      • Expected benefits and potential risks.
      • Alternative options, including no treatment.
    2. Voluntary Consent – Consent must be freely given without pressure.
    3. Documentation – Written consent will be obtained via the patient intake form and recorded in the patient’s notes.
    4. Ongoing Consent – Consent is not a one-time event; patients can withdraw or modify consent at any point during treatment.
    5. Capacity – If a patient lacks capacity to consent, treatment may only proceed with a legally authorised representative.
    Statement for Patients:

    "I have received information about my assessment and treatment, understand the risks and benefits and agree to receive physiotherapy care."

  3. Child Consent

    Definition:

    Children are individuals under 18 years of age. Parental/guardian consent is required.

    Policy:
    1. Parental/Guardian Consent – A parent or legal guardian must provide consent before the child is assessed or treated.
    2. Child Assent – Where possible, the child’s understanding and willingness to participate will be sought in age-appropriate language.
    3. Information Provision – The physiotherapist will explain to the parent/guardian and the child:
      • The assessment and treatment plan.
      • Expected benefits and potential risks.
      • Alternatives, including the option not to treat.
    4. Documentation – Consent will be recorded on a child consent form and included in the patient record.
    5. Ongoing Consent – Parents/guardians and children can withdraw consent at any time.
    Statement for Parents/Guardians:

    "I am the parent/legal guardian of [child’s name]. I have received information about the proposed assessment and treatment, understand the potential risks and benefits, and give consent for my child to receive physiotherapy care."

  4. Review and Updates

    • This policy is reviewed annually or when there are changes in legislation, clinical guidelines, or clinic procedures.
    • All staff are trained to follow this consent policy and to answer questions from patients or guardians.

  5. Responsibilities

    • Physiotherapists: Ensure informed consent is obtained and documented.
    • Reception/Admin Staff: Provide consent forms and explain procedures to patients/guardians.
    • Clinic Owner/Manager: Monitor adherence to this policy and provide staff training.

Incident Reporting Policy

Policy Statement

Buzzy Bees Physiotherapy is committed to providing a safe environment for our patients, staff and visitors. Reporting incidents promptly ensures that we can take corrective action, prevent recurrence and comply with relevant health and safety regulations.

Purpose

This policy establishes a clear and consistent process for reporting, recording and managing all incidents, including patient injuries, staff injuries, near misses and property damage.

  1. Scope

    This policy applies to all employees, contractors, volunteers, and students working within the clinic. It covers:

    • Patient-related incidents (falls, adverse reactions, treatment errors).
    • Staff-related incidents (injuries, exposure to hazards).
    • Environmental or property incidents (equipment faults, slip/trip hazards).
    • Near misses or potentially unsafe situations.

  2. Definitions

    • Incident: Any event that causes harm or has the potential to cause harm.
    • Near Miss: An event that could have caused harm but did not.
    • Serious Incident: Any incident that results in hospitalisation, significant injury, or major equipment/property damage.

  3. Responsibilities

    All Staff:
    • Report incidents immediately to the supervisor or manager.
    • Complete the incident report form accurately and promptly.
    Clinic Manager / Supervisor:
    • Ensure incidents are logged and investigated.
    • Notify relevant authorities if required (e.g. RIDDOR).
    • Follow up to implement corrective actions and prevent recurrence.
    • Maintain incident records.
    • Review trends and provide recommendations for improvements.

  4. Incident Reporting Procedure

    1. Immediate Action – Ensure the safety of all involved, provide first aid if necessary and secure the area.
    2. Report – Notify the Clinic Manager or Supervisor immediately.
    3. Document – Complete an Incident Report Form, including:
      • Date, time and location.
      • Individuals involved.
      • Description of what happened.
      • Immediate actions taken.
      • Witnesses (if any).
    4. Investigation – Clinic Manager reviews the incident and determines:
      • Cause.
      • Preventative measures.
      • Whether external reporting is required.
    5. Follow-Up – Implement corrective measures and communicate lessons learned to staff.
    6. Record Keeping – Store incident reports securely for a minimum of 3 years (or as required by law).

  5. Confidentiality

    All incident reports are treated confidentially. Information is shared only with relevant staff, regulatory bodies or insurers as necessary.

  6. Training

    All staff must have read this policy during induction and receive annual refreshers on incident identification, reporting and management.

  7. Review

    This policy will be reviewed annually, or after a significant incident, to ensure it remains effective and compliant with legislation.

Moving and Handling Policy

Policy Statement

At Buzzy Bees Physiotherapy, we are committed to ensuring the health, safety and wellbeing of our staff, patients and visitors. Manual handling tasks can pose significant risks if not performed safely. This policy sets out our approach to preventing injury through safe handling practices and compliance with the Health and Safety Executive (HSE) guidelines.

  1. Purpose

    The purpose of this policy is to:

    • Protect staff and patients from injury related to manual handling activities.
    • Provide clear guidance on safe handling procedures.
    • Comply with relevant health and safety legislation.

  2. Scope

    This policy applies to all staff, subcontractors and volunteers who may be required to lift, move, or support patients, equipment or supplies within the clinic or during community visits.

  3. Responsibilities

    Clinic Owner / Manager:
    • Ensure that risk assessments for manual handling are carried out.
    • Provide appropriate training and equipment.
    • Monitor compliance with safe handling procedures.
    Staff:
    • Follow the manual handling procedures outlined in this policy.
    • Report any incidents, near misses or concerns immediately.
    • Attend all required training sessions.

  4. Risk Assessment

    • Identify tasks that involve manual handling (lifting, transferring, supporting, repositioning patients or equipment).
    • Assess the risks associated with each task, including patient weight, mobility and environmental factors.
    • Implement control measures to minimise risks, such as using mechanical aids or adjusting patient positioning.

  5. Safe Handling Procedures

    1. Planning – Assess the task before moving. Identify hazards and plan the lift.
    2. Positioning – Maintain a stable stance, keep feet shoulder-width apart and bend knees—not the back.
    3. Lifting & Moving – Use smooth movements, avoid twisting, keep the load close to your body.
    4. Use of Equipment – Utilise hoists, slide sheets, transfer belts or trolleys whenever possible.
    5. Team Lifting – Ensure adequate staff are available and coordinate movements verbally.
    6. Patient Participation – Encourage patients to assist with movements where safely possible.

  6. Training

    • All staff will have up to date manual handling training.
    • Training will cover correct lifting techniques, use of mechanical aids and patient transfer protocols.

  7. Incident Reporting

    • Any manual handling injury or near miss must be reported immediately to the manager.
    • An investigation will be conducted to prevent recurrence and adjustments to procedures or equipment will be made if necessary.

  8. Monitoring & Review

    • This policy will be reviewed annually or following a significant incident.
    • Feedback from staff will be used to improve manual handling procedures continuously.

Safeguarding Policy

Effective Date

18/02/2026

Review Date

18/02/2027

  1. Policy Statement

    Buzzy Bees Physiotherapy is committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of children, young people, and adults at risk.

    We recognise our duty of care to protect individuals from abuse, neglect, exploitation, and harm in all settings where we deliver services, including:

    • Client homes.
    • Clinics.
    • Schools.
    • Community venues.
    • Hydrotherapy pools.
    • Outdoor rehabilitation settings.

    Safeguarding is everyone’s responsibility. All staff, contractors, and associates must understand their role in recognising and responding to safeguarding concerns.

  2. Scope

    This policy applies to:

    • Physiotherapists.
    • Rehabilitation Assistants.
    • Administrative staff.
    • Students.
    • Contractors.
    • Volunteers.

    It covers both children (under 18) and adults at risk (as defined by the Care Act 2014).

  3. Definitions

    Child

    Any person under the age of 18.

    Adult at Risk

    An adult who:

    • Has care and support needs.
    • Is experiencing, or at risk of, abuse or neglect.
    • Is unable to protect themselves because of those needs.

  4. Types of Abuse

    Safeguarding concerns may include (but are not limited to):

    Children and Adults
    • Physical abuse.
    • Emotional or psychological abuse.
    • Sexual abuse.
    • Neglect or acts of omission.
    • Financial or material abuse.
    • Discriminatory abuse.
    • Domestic abuse.
    • Modern slavery.
    • Exploitation (including criminal or sexual exploitation).
    • Online abuse.

    Staff must remain vigilant to signs including unexplained injuries, behavioural changes, withdrawal, fearfulness, or disclosure.

  5. Safer Recruitment

    We are committed to safer recruitment practices, including:

    • Enhanced DBS checks where appropriate.
    • Verification of identity and qualifications.
    • Professional registration checks (HCPC).
    • References prior to employment.
    • Ongoing professional supervision.

  6. Responsibilities

    Safeguarding Lead

    The Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) is:

    Name: Claire Williamson

    Role: Director

    Contact: info@buzzybeesphysio.co.uk

    The DSL is responsible for:

    • Receiving safeguarding concerns.
    • Making referrals to local authority safeguarding teams.
    • Liaising with schools, social care, or police where required.
    • Maintaining safeguarding records.
    • Ensuring staff training compliance.

    If the DSL is unavailable and there is immediate risk, staff must contact emergency services (999) or the relevant local authority safeguarding team directly.

  7. Recognising and Responding to Concerns

    If a child or adult discloses abuse:
    • Stay calm.
    • Listen carefully.
    • Do not promise confidentiality.
    • Reassure them they have done the right thing.
    • Do not investigate.
    • Record facts accurately and objectively.
    If you observe signs of harm:
    • Document factual observations.
    • Report immediately to the DSL.
    If someone is in immediate danger:
    • Call 999.
    • Inform the DSL as soon as possible.

  8. Reporting Procedure

    1. Immediate Risk – Contact emergency services.
    2. Report Concern – Report the concern to the Designated Safeguarding Lead (Claire Williamson).
    3. Assessment and Referral – The DSL assesses the concern and refers to:
      • Local Authority Children’s Services.
      • Adult Safeguarding Team.
      • Police (if criminal offence is suspected).

    All concerns must be recorded within 24 hours.

  9. Working in Community Settings

    When working in homes, schools, or community venues:

    • Lone working risk assessments must be completed.
    • Staff must follow professional boundaries.
    • Visits must be documented.
    • Any environmental concerns (e.g., unsafe living conditions) must be reported.

    If staff feel unsafe, they should withdraw and inform management immediately.

  10. Professional Boundaries

    Staff (including subcontractors) must:

    • Maintain clear professional boundaries.
    • Avoid dual relationships.
    • Not accept significant gifts.
    • Not engage in personal social media contact with clients.

    Any concerns about boundary issues must be reported.

  11. Information Sharing

    Information will be shared on a need to know basis in accordance with:

    • UK GDPR.
    • Data Protection Act 2018.

    Safeguarding concerns override confidentiality where there is risk of harm.

    Consent will be sought where appropriate, but it is not required where there is risk of serious harm.

  12. Training

    All staff must:

    • Complete safeguarding training appropriate to their role.
    • Undertake refresher training at least every 2–3 years.
    • Receive safeguarding induction.

    The DSL should undertake enhanced safeguarding training.

  13. Record Keeping

    Safeguarding records will be:

    • Factual and objective.
    • Dated and signed.
    • Stored securely.
    • Kept separate from general clinical notes where appropriate.

  14. Whistleblowing

    Staff who are concerned about unsafe practice or organisational failings may raise concerns internally with the DSL or management.

    If concerns are not addressed, staff may escalate to:

    • Local Authority Safeguarding Team.
    • HCPC.
    • NSPCC Whistleblowing Helpline.

    Staff will not suffer detriment for raising genuine concerns.

  15. Review

    This policy will be reviewed annually or sooner if legislation or guidance changes.
